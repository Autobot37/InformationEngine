 We deduced that the neocortex stores everything we know, all our knowledge, using something called reference frames

Reference frames are not an optional component of intelligence; they are the structure in which all information is stored in the brain

'Thinking' Consists of Moving Through Reference Frames

 Every day, many of the synapses on an individual neuron will disappear and new ones will replace them. Thus, much of learning occurs by forming new connections between neurons that were previously not connected. Forgetting happens when old or unused connections are removed entirely.
